# TyphooN-Terminal Licensing FAQ

## What license is TyphooN-Terminal under?

TyphooN-Terminal is licensed under the **Business Source License 1.1** (BSL).
The source code is publicly available on GitHub. After four years, each
version automatically converts to the **Apache License 2.0**.

## Can I use TyphooN-Terminal for free?

Yes, for most uses:

- **Personal trading** on your own accounts is always free, regardless of
  how much money you make trading.
- **Education and research** use is always free.
- **Development and testing** is always free.
- **Internal business use** (using it as a tool, not offering it to others
  as a product) is always free.
- **Commercial use below $100,000/year** in attributable revenue is free.

## When do I need a commercial license?

You need a commercial license if you incorporate TyphooN-Terminal into a
product or service offered to third parties that generates more than
$100,000 per year in attributable revenue.

Examples that require a commercial license:
- A broker repackaging TyphooN-Terminal as their trading platform
- A SaaS product built on TyphooN-Terminal's engine
- A fund offering TyphooN-Terminal as part of a managed trading service

## How much does a commercial license cost?

Royalties are graduated based on annual attributable revenue:

| Revenue Tier              | Rate                           |
|---------------------------|--------------------------------|
| $0 - $100,000             | Free (no license needed)       |
| $100,001 - $500,000       | 3% of revenue above $100K     |
| $500,001 - $2,000,000     | 5% of revenue above $500K     |
| $2,000,001 - $10,000,000  | 7% of revenue above $2M       |
| Above $10,000,000         | Negotiated                     |

## I'm a solo trader making money. Do I need a license?

No. Personal trading on your own accounts is always free. The commercial
license only applies when you offer TyphooN-Terminal as part of a product
or service to others.

## Can I fork and modify TyphooN-Terminal?

Yes. You can fork, modify, and use it under the BSL terms. If your use
stays within the Additional Use Grant (non-commercial or under $100K
revenue), no commercial license is needed.

## What happens after four years?

Each version converts to Apache 2.0 four years after publication. At that
point, you can use that version for any purpose with no restrictions.

## Why BSL instead of a traditional open-source license?

BSL protects the developer's ability to earn revenue from commercial users
while keeping the source code publicly available. It prevents large
companies from repackaging the software as their own product without
compensation, while ensuring personal and small-scale users always have
free access.
